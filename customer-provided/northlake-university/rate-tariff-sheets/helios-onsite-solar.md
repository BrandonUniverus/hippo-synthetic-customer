# Agreement Sheet — Helios Onsite Solar (Power Purchase Agreement)

Provided by: Northlake University Finance + Sustainability. Synthetic/illustrative
values for setup. Helios Onsite Solar is a **third-party developer** that owns
rooftop and carport photovoltaic arrays on our properties and sells the output
under a **Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)**. Billing is **per kWh generated**, not
per kWh consumed, and there is no demand charge. Generation offsets our Valley
Electric load; surplus is exported for a net-metering credit.

## Agreement: Solar Power Purchase Agreement (HOS-PPA-2024)

**Effective Jan 1, 2024 – Dec 31, 2025**

| Item | Basis | Value |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PPA energy | per kWh generated | $0.119 |
| Annual escalator | per year | 2.0% |
| Net-metering credit | per kWh exported | $0.092 |
| Renewable energy certificates (RECs) | ownership | **Northlake retains** |

The monthly PPA invoice = PPA energy charge − net-metering credit. Helios also
issues a monthly REC and avoided-emissions statement (informational; no charge).

**Arrays (PPA agreements by site):** Science Center, Student Center, Cedar Row A
carport.

**Notes for setup:**
- Generation is **positive-generation / negative-load** — it reduces our net
  Valley Electric usage on shared buildings.
- Output follows the daily solar shape, peaks midday, near zero overnight.
- One inverter outage leaves a generation gap that is later backfilled.
- We **retain the RECs** for market-based emissions reporting (see Sustainability
  Requirements) — please reflect this in environmental accounting.
- **Open:** confirm which solar attributes Northlake retains under the PPA.
