# Rate Sheet — Northlake Thermal Plant (Internal Cost Allocation)

Provided by: Northlake University Finance + Thermal Plant. Synthetic/illustrative
values for setup. The thermal plant is **university-owned** — there is no external
utility account. It produces chilled water (cooling) and steam (heating) and
**allocates its operating cost** to the buildings it serves, using internal cost
centers and submetered consumption.

**Effective Jan 1, 2024 – Dec 31, 2025**

## Chilled Water Cost Allocation (NTP-ALLOC-CHW)

| Charge | Basis | Rate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Consumption | per ton-hour | $0.168 |
| Demand | per ton per month (peak) | $9.50 |

Served (sub-metered) building: Science Center (hourly ton-hours and tons).

## Steam Cost Allocation (NTP-ALLOC-STEAM)

| Charge | Basis | Rate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Consumption | per klb (thousand lb steam) | $14.20 |

Served buildings: Science Center, Admin Hall, Library, Student Center.

**Notes for setup:**
- These behave like monthly "bills" against internal **cost centers**, not utility
  accounts — please roll them up alongside utility costs.
- The plant is itself a Valley Electric customer (chiller power) and a Sierra Gas
  customer (boiler fuel), so its emissions tie to delivered thermal energy.
- Cooling demand is seasonal and peaks on summer afternoons.
- **Open:** confirm the allocation rule for steam-only buildings.
